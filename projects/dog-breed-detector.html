Dog Breed Detector

This Android app scans a dog in view of the phone's camera and determines its best guesses as to what the dog's breed is. When a dog is determined to be in the camera's view, a series of 10 "scans" are taken, where each scan results in the model returning the probability that the dog is of each breed. When the 10 scans are completed the scores are averaged and the user can view the top few guesses for the dog's breed. The app was made in Android Studio and uses a Tensorflow implementation of the Mobilenet model that was trained with the Stanford Dogs Dataset. It thus has the capacity to distinguish 120 dog breeds.
